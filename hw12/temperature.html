Latitude vs. Temperature

This chart shows what most of know intuitively — that locations closer to the equator are, on average, warmer than those farther from the equator. The correlation speaks for itself here, as we see temperatures peaking near the equator (0 degrees latitude) and becoming colder the farther we move from that point in either direction. One point of interest is the apprent difference between the Northern and Southern hemispheres, as we see a more dramatic dropoff north of the equator than we do moving south. This could be due to a larger number of cities reporting from the Northern Hemisphere than from the Southern Hemisphere, or from a larger number of cities existing farther to the north than to the south.
